## Case outcome 1 — US B2B SaaS (Series A, multi-channel demand gen)

**Context**

- GA4 and CRM channel numbers did not match.
- Direct + Unassigned represented a large share of conversion reporting.
- Marketing and leadership did not trust weekly performance dashboards.

**What was done**

- Introduced strict UTM governance and naming controls.
- Fixed attribution resets across website and booking flow.
- Standardized CRM source capture fields at conversion time.

**Outcome (6 weeks)**

- Unassigned share reduced by ~60% relative to baseline.
- Channel reporting variance between GA4 and CRM dropped materially.
- Team moved to one weekly source-of-truth reporting view for budget decisions.

## Case outcome 2 — UK B2B Fintech (inbound + paid social + search)

**Context**

- Lead volume was acceptable, but SQL quality by channel was unclear.
- Paid spend allocation was mostly platform-reported, not pipeline-validated.

**What was done**

- Rebuilt event model around intent (not vanity actions).
- Added booked-call and quality proxies into GA4/GTM tracking.
- Mapped CRM stages to a practical decision dashboard.

**Outcome (8-10 weeks)**

- Booked-call to opportunity consistency improved notably.
- Low-quality channel spend was reduced and reallocated to higher-yield paths.
- Leadership gained confidence in weekly channel decision-making.

## Case outcome 3 — Canadian B2B Services (long sales cycle)

**Context**

- Strong top-of-funnel activity but weak attribution confidence.
- Revenue conversations were detached from acquisition signals.

**What was done**

- Implemented minimum viable attribution framework across GA4, GTM, and CRM.
- Added recurring QA cadence for source/medium integrity and conversion logic.
- Built operator-friendly scorecard for ongoing weekly use.

**Outcome (first quarter)**

- Fewer reporting disputes between marketing and sales.
- Faster decision cycles for campaign optimization.
- More consistent link between acquisition reporting and pipeline planning.
